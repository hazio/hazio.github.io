# Resolution Measurement Framework (RMF)

## A Proposed Measurement Architecture for Signal Resolution Within SRF

### Status

Conceptual framework.

Not empirically validated.

Proposed as a candidate operationalization layer for Structural Regulation Framework (SRF).

---

# Core Problem

SRF proposes that psychological suffering often emerges from domain-specific reductions in representational resolution.

However:

> How do we know whether resolution has actually increased?

Current approaches rely heavily on:

- self-report
- insight
- subjective improvement
- symptom reduction

These are useful but insufficient.

A person may:

- understand their history perfectly
- explain their patterns accurately
- describe their trauma eloquently

while remaining highly compressed within the relevant signal domain.

Therefore:

> Resolution requires direct operationalization.

---

# Core Definition

Within SRF:

**Resolution** refers to the capacity to maintain differentiated, multidimensional representations under activation without collapsing into simplified defensive encoding.

Resolution is not:

- intelligence
- verbal ability
- psychological knowledge
- insight

Resolution is:

> representational differentiation under load.

---

# Measurement Principle

Resolution should not be measured in calm states.

Compression typically emerges under activation.

Therefore:

> Resolution must be measured under signal load.

---

# Candidate Dimensions

## 1. Differentiation Capacity (DC)

Measures:

How many distinct elements remain available within an activated domain?

Examples:

Low resolution:

- "I am bad."
- "Everything is ruined."

High resolution:

- shame
- disappointment
- sadness
- frustration
- fear
- uncertainty

remain distinguishable.

### Candidate Metric

Count of differentiated emotional, cognitive, and interpersonal representations maintained during activation.

---

## 2. Contradiction Holding Capacity (CHC)

Measures:

Ability to maintain seemingly contradictory representations simultaneously.

Examples:

Low resolution:

- "If I made a mistake, I am worthless."

High resolution:

- "I made a mistake."
- "I remain valuable."

Both can coexist.

### Candidate Metric

Number and complexity of contradictory representations tolerated without collapse.

---

## 3. Context Preservation Capacity (CPC)

Measures:

Ability to maintain contextual information during activation.

Examples:

Low resolution:

- isolated event becomes total reality

High resolution:

- current event remains embedded within larger context

### Candidate Metric

Degree to which historical, relational, and situational context remains accessible during stress.

---

## 4. Perspective Multiplicity Capacity (PMC)

Measures:

Ability to generate multiple plausible interpretations.

Low resolution:

- one interpretation

High resolution:

- several simultaneous interpretations

Example:

Low resolution:

"He ignored me because I am unimportant."

High resolution:

Possible explanations include:

- distraction
- stress
- misunderstanding
- conflict
- intentional distancing

### Candidate Metric

Number of viable interpretations maintained without premature closure.

---

## 5. Self–Other Differentiation Capacity (SODC)

Measures:

Ability to separate self-representation from external evaluation.

Low resolution:

- criticism = self-collapse

High resolution:

- criticism remains information

### Candidate Metric

Degree of representational separation maintained under evaluative activation.

---

# Resolution Stress Testing

Resolution should be measured dynamically.

---

## Baseline Condition

Participant evaluates emotionally neutral stimuli.

Establishes baseline differentiation.

---

## Activation Condition

Participant encounters signal-class-specific activation.

Examples:

- shame activation
- rejection activation
- dependency activation
- status threat activation
- ambiguity activation

---

## Escalation Condition

Activation intensity gradually increases.

Goal:

Identify the point at which resolution collapses.

---

# Compression Threshold (CT)

Definition:

The activation intensity at which differentiated representation becomes unavailable.

Example:

At low activation:

- shame
- sadness
- anger
- disappointment

remain distinct.

At threshold:

Everything becomes:

- "I am worthless."

Compression Threshold marks the transition.

---

# Resolution Profile

Different signal classes may show different thresholds.

Example:

| Signal Class | Compression Threshold |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Shame | Low |
| Dependency | Medium |
| Status | High |
| Ambiguity | Medium |
| Rejection | Very Low |

This creates a personal Resolution Profile.

---

# Resolution Index (RI)

Candidate composite score:

RI = f(
Differentiation Capacity,
Contradiction Holding Capacity,
Context Preservation Capacity,
Perspective Multiplicity Capacity,
Self–Other Differentiation Capacity
)

Higher values indicate greater maintained resolution under activation.

---

# Longitudinal Measurement

RMF allows measurement of change over time.

Questions:

- Has resolution increased?
- Has compression threshold shifted?
- Has differentiation improved?
- Has contradiction tolerance improved?

This provides a potential alternative to purely symptom-based evaluation.

---

# Relationship to SRF

RMF operationalizes a central SRF construct.

Paper 1:
- REL measures timing of externalization

Paper 4:
- Compression Profiles describe where compression occurs

Paper 5:
- Self Architecture describes what is protected

Paper 6:
- Ontological Regulation describes deepest stakes

RMF attempts to measure:

> How much representational resolution remains available under activation.

---

# Key Prediction

Individuals with similar symptom profiles may possess dramatically different resolution architectures.

Likewise:

Symptom reduction may occur without meaningful resolution growth.

Conversely:

Resolution growth may precede observable symptom reduction.

---

# Limitations

Current framework is entirely conceptual.

Major open questions:

- Can resolution be measured reliably?
- How can verbal intelligence be separated from resolution?
- Are different dimensions independent?
- Is resolution domain-specific or partially global?
- How stable are compression thresholds across time?

These questions require empirical investigation.

---

# Foundational Hypothesis

Psychological growth may not primarily consist of acquiring insight.

Psychological growth may consist of increasing the capacity to maintain differentiated representation in domains that previously collapsed under activation.

If so, the central question is not:

> "Do you understand your problem?"

but:

> "Can you maintain resolution when the problem becomes emotionally real?"
