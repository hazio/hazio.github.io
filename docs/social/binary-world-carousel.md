## Caption

```
The world isn't black and white because you want it to be.

It becomes binary because your system can't hold both at the same time.

Most people think splitting is a choice — or a character flaw. It's neither.

It's what happens when the system encounters contradiction it cannot hold. The only options become: all good, or all bad. Because "both" requires a structural capacity that isn't there yet.

Idealization = contradiction eliminated in the positive direction.
Devaluation = contradiction eliminated in the negative direction.

Not a flaw. A compression algorithm.

The beginning of integration isn't insight. It's survived contradiction. The first moment where both can coexist — and the system doesn't collapse.
```
