## 2. Internal theoretical integration

### REL × Compression Profile linkage

Important unresolved internal question:

Is REL:

global?

or

signal-class dependent?

**Decision (frozen):**

REL is introduced in Paper 1 as a global construct for theoretical parsimony. However, the theory program now acknowledges that effective externalization latency likely varies across signal classes — a person may tolerate professional frustration for hours while externalizing shame-related activation within seconds.

The canonical position is:

> REL is defined globally in Paper 1 as a first-order approximation. Signal-class-specific REL variation is acknowledged as theoretically likely and empirically testable, but is not formally parameterized in the current theory. Whether REL proves to be a single latent variable with signal-class modulation or a family of signal-class-specific latencies remains an open empirical question.

This parallels the IRC treatment: global in Paper 1, signal-class-specific acknowledged in Papers 2 and 4.

No paper rewrite required. Paper 1 Section 13.11 already acknowledges global parameter simplification.

Assessment:

**Resolved architecturally. Empirical question remains open.**

## 7. Defensive Reality Reconstruction (Paper 7 candidate — under consideration)

Missing question:

**What happens when reality itself becomes too expensive to represent accurately?**

Papers 1–6 explain compression (resolution loss) but not reconstruction (active reality rewriting). These are different: compression = "I don't see clearly." Reconstruction = "I see something else."

Core idea:

When representational cost exceeds available integration capacity, the system may reconstruct accessible subjective reality to preserve regulatory stability. This is not deliberate deception — it is structural defensive reconstruction where altered narrative becomes accessible truth.

Key construct: **Representational Cost** — the psychological cost of accurately representing a given reality state.

Critical risk: substantial overlap with motivated reasoning (Kunda, 1990), cognitive dissonance (Festinger, 1957), and self-deception literature. Novelty depends on structural integration with Papers 1–6 — SRF explains *why* specific truths are too costly (self architecture + compression profiles), not merely *that* bias occurs.

Status: **Under consideration.** Not part of first-generation theory (Papers 1–6). Second-generation expansion if novelty can be clearly established vs. existing motivated cognition literature.

Assessment:

Genuine explanatory gap exists. But priority is lower than empirical validation of Papers 1–6.

## Frozen Terminology Registry (CANONICAL — do not drift)

| Term | Exact definition | Paper introduced | DO NOT confuse with |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IRC** | Maximum holding threshold for unresolved activation without discharge. Dynamic, state-modifiable, structurally constrained. | Paper 1 | Energy, resource, fuel, trait |
| **SF** | Capacity to hold tension, contradiction, and reality contact while shifting strategies without fragmentation | Paper 1 | Cognitive flexibility, intelligence, mentalization |
| **ERD** | Degree to which stable self-coherence depends on external regulatory input | Paper 1 | Attachment need, dependency trait, neediness |
| **REL** | Time interval between unresolved activation and initiation of external regulatory behavior | Paper 1 | Impulsivity, response latency, reaction time |
| **Representational Resolution** | Degree to which a signal can be affectively, interpretively, and symbolically differentiated. Higher-order composite. | Paper 4 | Bit depth (metaphor only), intelligence, cognitive complexity |
| **Compression** | Defensive reduction in representational resolution under activation. Structural, not random, not cognitive load. | Paper 4 | Suppression, avoidance, alexithymia, cognitive simplification |
| **Compression Profile** | Characteristic distribution of effective resolution across canonical signal classes | Paper 4 | Personality type, diagnosis, global trait |
| **Signal Class** | Psychologically meaningful category of experience requiring interpretation and regulation | Paper 4 | Context, domain, situation, emotion |
| **Self Architecture** | Structural organization of the self determining what kinds of reality can be integrated without collapse | Paper 5 | Self-concept content, self-esteem, personality |
| **Worth Contingency** | Degree to which self-worth depends on specific domains remaining intact | Paper 5 | Self-esteem level, narcissism, contingent self-worth (broader) |
| **Ontological Self-Continuity** | Degree to which felt sense of existing persists independently of external confirmation | Paper 5/6 | Self-esteem, identity clarity, attachment security |
| **Continuity of being** | Felt experiential sense of existing as a real, continuous self (Winnicott-derived) | Paper 6 | Existence (too metaphysical), identity (too narrative) |
| **Regulatory stakes** | Level at which a signal threatens the system: affective / identity / ontological | Paper 5/6 | Severity, intensity, importance |
| **Full-Resolution Cost** | Psychological cost of accurately representing a given reality state at full resolution | Manifesto (not yet in papers) | REL (different construct entirely) |
| **Resolution Resistance** | Persistence of compressed processing because decompression would exceed holding capacity | Paper 2 | Motivational resistance, stubbornness, denial |
| **Scaffolding** | Temporary external augmentation of holding threshold during structural transition | Paper 2 | Therapy (too broad), support (too vague) |

### Terminology rules

1. **Never use "binary"** in formal paper text — use "highly compressed dichotomous processing" or "categorical"
2. **Never use "primitive"** — use "minimally differentiated" or "low-resolution"
3. **Never use "existence"** for ontological stakes — use "continuity of being"
4. **Never use "energy" or "fuel"** for IRC — use "holding threshold" or "holding capacity"
5. **Never use "This is not a metaphor"** — use "proposed as a structural hypothesis" or "provisionally operationalizable"
6. **Never use "explains"** without hedging in speculative papers — use "may help explain" or "offers one structural interpretation"
7. **REL = Regulation Externalization Latency ONLY** — never reuse acronym for other constructs
