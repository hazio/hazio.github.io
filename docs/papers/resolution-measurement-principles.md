# Resolution Measurement Principles

## Four constraints for operationalizing representational resolution within SRF

---

## Principle 1: Resolution must be measured under activation

Resolution measured in calm states reflects verbal ability, psychological knowledge, or rehearsed narrative - not structural capacity.

A person may describe their shame patterns with extraordinary nuance during a relaxed interview while collapsing into categorical processing the moment shame is actually activated.

Therefore: any valid resolution measure must include signal-class-specific activation as a measurement condition. Baseline measurement without activation measures something, but not resolution in the SRF sense.

---

## Principle 2: Resolution is not equivalent to insight

Insight is knowledge about one's patterns. Resolution is the capacity to maintain differentiated representation while those patterns are active.

A person may have complete insight ("I know I withdraw when I feel dependent") while remaining fully compressed in the relevant domain under activation. Insight without resolution is understanding without structural change.

Resolution measurement must therefore be independent of self-reported understanding.

---

## Principle 3: Resolution is not equivalent to verbal complexity

Articulate people can describe compressed experience in complex language. Verbal sophistication may mask compression rather than indicate resolution.

The question is not: "Can this person talk about shame in nuanced ways?"

The question is: "Can this person maintain differentiated representation of shame-relevant signals while shame is activated?"

These are different capacities. Resolution measurement must control for verbal intelligence.

---

## Principle 4: Compression Threshold is the primary candidate metric

Compression Threshold (CT) is defined as:

> The activation intensity at which differentiated representation becomes unavailable within a specific signal class.

CT may be to resolution what REL is to externalization:

- Simple
- Measurable
- Comparable across individuals
- Signal-class-specific
- Trackable over time

CT produces a single number per signal class: the activation level at which resolution collapses. A person's CT profile across signal classes constitutes their empirical compression profile.

### Why CT may be the entry point

CT does not require measuring "how much" resolution exists (complex, multidimensional). It requires measuring "at what point" resolution disappears (simpler, threshold-based).

This is analogous to measuring breaking point rather than material composition. You do not need to fully characterize the material - you need to know when it fails.

### Candidate paradigm

1. Establish baseline differentiation in a signal domain (neutral condition)
2. Gradually increase activation intensity (escalation condition)
3. Identify the point at which differentiated responding collapses into categorical processing
4. That point = Compression Threshold for that signal class

### What CT enables

- Individual CT profiles across signal classes (empirical compression profiles)
- Longitudinal tracking (has CT shifted after therapy?)
- Cross-individual comparison (same signal class, different thresholds)
- Treatment specificity (which interventions shift CT in which domains?)

---

## Strategic position

These four principles constrain future measurement development without committing to a specific instrument.

They establish what resolution measurement must do (activate, not rely on insight or verbal skill, identify threshold) without yet specifying how.

The next step is a concrete pilot protocol for CT measurement in one signal class.
