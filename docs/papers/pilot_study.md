# Shame Compression Pilot Study

## A Pilot Protocol for Measuring Compression Threshold and Compression Curves in the Shame Domain

### Status

Concept proposal.

Pilot study design.

Intended as a feasibility study for the Dynamic Stability Framework (DSF).

---

# Background

The Structural Regulation Framework (SRF) proposes that psychological functioning depends partly on the ability to maintain differentiated representation under activation.

The Dynamic Stability Framework (DSF) extends this proposal by introducing three candidate measurement constructs:

- Compression Threshold (CT)
- Compression Curve (CC)
- Dynamic Stability Profile (DSP)

The present study does not test the entirety of SRF.

Instead, it evaluates a narrower question:

> Can representational resolution in a psychologically significant signal domain be measured dynamically under activation?

The shame domain was selected because:

- shame is clinically significant
- shame appears strongly linked to self-architecture
- shame is expected to produce substantial individual differences
- shame may exhibit both gradual and catastrophic compression dynamics

---

# Research Question

Can Compression Threshold (CT) and Compression Curve (CC) be identified within the shame domain?

---

# Primary Objectives

1. Determine whether differentiated representation decreases under increasing shame activation.

2. Identify whether individual participants exhibit distinguishable Compression Thresholds.

3. Explore whether Compression Curves display recognizable patterns:

- Linear
- Threshold
- Stepwise
- Catastrophic

4. Evaluate the feasibility of a larger empirical research program.

---

# Hypotheses

## H1

Differentiated representation decreases as shame activation increases.

---

## H2

Compression Thresholds differ between individuals.

---

## H3

Compression Curves differ between individuals.

---

## H4

Some participants exhibit abrupt compression dynamics consistent with catastrophic curve profiles.

---

# Participants

### Pilot Sample

N = 20–30 adults

Age:

18–65

Recruitment:

- volunteers
- community recruitment
- university participant pools

No diagnostic selection criteria required for pilot phase.

---

# Study Design

Within-subject repeated activation design.

Each participant progresses through multiple activation levels.

Representational differentiation is measured after each activation step.

---

# Procedure

## Phase 1: Baseline

Participants complete a neutral autobiographical recall task.

Example:

> Describe a typical grocery shopping experience.

Goal:

Establish baseline differentiation capacity.

Measures:

- emotional differentiation
- contextual differentiation
- perspective differentiation

---

## Phase 2: Mild Shame Activation

Prompt:

> Recall a situation in which you felt mildly embarrassed or inadequate.

Participants describe:

- emotions
- thoughts
- interpretations
- perceived causes

Differentiation is coded.

---

## Phase 3: Moderate Shame Activation

Prompt:

> Recall a situation in which you felt significant shame, rejection, or personal failure.

Participants repeat the same descriptive task.

Differentiation is coded.

---

## Phase 4: High Shame Activation

Prompt:

> Recall a personally meaningful experience involving intense shame, rejection, humiliation, or perceived inadequacy.

Optional guided imagery procedures may be used.

Participants again provide descriptions.

Differentiation is coded.

---

## Phase 5: Recovery

Participants complete:

- emotional debriefing
- grounding exercises
- post-session check-in

---

# Measurement

## Representational Differentiation Coding

At each activation level, participant responses are coded for:

### Emotional Differentiation

Number of distinct emotional states identified.

Examples:

- shame
- sadness
- disappointment
- fear
- anger
- loneliness

---

### Contextual Differentiation

Extent to which contextual information remains accessible.

Examples:

- situational factors
- relationship factors
- historical factors
- alternative explanations

---

### Perspective Differentiation

Ability to maintain multiple viewpoints simultaneously.

Examples:

- self perspective
- other perspective
- observer perspective

---

# Compression Threshold (CT)

Operational Definition:

> The activation level at which differentiated representation can no longer be reliably maintained.

Indicators:

- sudden reduction in emotional differentiation
- collapse into global statements
- loss of contextual complexity
- reduction in alternative interpretations

Examples:

High resolution:

> I felt ashamed, disappointed, afraid of rejection, and angry at myself.

Compressed representation:

> I am a failure.

---

# Compression Curve (CC)

Compression Curves are derived from differentiation scores across activation levels.

Candidate patterns:

### Linear

Gradual decline.

---

### Threshold

Stable differentiation followed by rapid decline.

---

### Stepwise

Multiple identifiable compression stages.

---

### Catastrophic

Abrupt large-scale collapse with minimal intermediate degradation.

# Data Analysis

Primary analyses:

- within-subject differentiation change
- between-subject CT variation
- exploratory CC classification

Visualization:

Differentiation scores plotted against activation level.

Each participant produces an individual Compression Curve.

---

# Feasibility Outcomes

Evidence supporting DSF feasibility would include:

- measurable differentiation decline
- identifiable Compression Thresholds
- distinguishable Compression Curve patterns
- acceptable inter-rater coding reliability

---

# Limitations

This pilot study does not establish:

- validity of SRF
- validity of DSF
- existence of stable signal-class architectures

The study only evaluates whether:

> representational compression under activation can be operationalized and measured.

---

# Expected Contribution

If successful, this pilot would provide the first empirical test of the central DSF assumption:

> Representational resolution changes systematically under activation and these changes can be quantified.

A positive result would justify larger studies investigating:

- signal-class specificity
- Dynamic Stability Profiles (DSP)
- Ontological Coupling
- therapeutic change as Compression Curve modification
