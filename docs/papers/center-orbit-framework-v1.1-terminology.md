# Structural Regulation Framework
## A Model of Internal and External Regulation Dynamics

**Version 1.1 — Terminology Revision**

---

## Abstract

The Structural Regulation Framework proposes a dimensional model of human psychological regulation organized around two orthogonal axes: Internal Regulation Capacity (IRC) and Structural Flexibility (SF). The framework models how individuals maintain psychological coherence under unresolved activation, and through which channels externalization occurs when internal capacity is exceeded.

The model introduces Regulation Externalization Latency (REL) as a dynamic process variable measuring the speed of regulatory externalization, and proposes externalization vectors describing the directional channels through which external regulation operates.

The framework adopts a probabilistic developmental model consistent with behavioral genetic evidence showing negligible shared familial environmental contribution to regulation-related traits (Back et al., 2026), and proposes that regulation organization emerges through interaction between genetic predisposition, temperament, and individual-specific environmental experiences.

# 1. Terminological Architecture

## 1.1 Design Principles

1. Each term refers to one and only one construct
2. No moral or aspirational connotation
3. Mechanistic rather than metaphorical language
4. Orthogonal structure between constructs preserved

## 1.2 Core Terms

| Term | Abbreviation | Definition | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Internal Regulation Capacity | IRC | Maximum capacity to maintain regulatory coherence internally under unresolved activation | Capacity |
| External Regulation Dependence | ERD | Degree to which psychological stability requires external regulatory input | Architecture |
| Structural Flexibility | SF | Capacity to hold tension, contradiction, and reality contact while shifting between regulatory strategies without fragmentation or rigidity | Capacity |
| Regulation Externalization Latency | REL | Time interval between unresolved activation and initiation of external regulatory behavior | Process |
| Externalization Vector | EV | Specific channel or motive through which external regulation operates | Strategy |

## 1.3 Derived Terms

| Term | Definition | Composition |
|---|---|---|
| Rigid Internal Regulation (RIR) | Internally located regulation maintained through suppression rather than flexibility | High IRC + Low SF |
| Compulsive External Dependence (CED) | Persistent inability to maintain coherence without external input | Low IRC + Low SF + High ERD |
| Adaptive External Engagement (AEE) | Chosen external regulation without identity dependence | High IRC + High SF + contextual ERD |
| Integrated Internal Regulation (IIR) | Internally located, flexible, reality-contacting regulation | High IRC + High SF |

## 1.4 Relationship Between IRC and SF

- **IRC** answers: "How much activation can the system hold internally?"
- **SF** answers: "How flexibly can the system process what it holds?"

A system may have:
- High IRC + Low SF = can hold tension but cannot transform it (rigid)
- Low IRC + High SF = flexible but overwhelmed easily
- High IRC + High SF = integrated regulation
- Low IRC + Low SF = compulsive externalization

SF is constrained by IRC (cannot flexibly process what cannot be held) but not reducible to it.

## 1.5 Mapping to Previous Terminology

| Previous (v1.0) | New (v1.1) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| CENTER | IRC | Removes moral connotation, narrows to capacity |
| ORBIT | ERD | Removes moral connotation, specifies dependence |
| Integration | SF | Removes overlap with IRC, specifies flexibility |
| Pseudo-CENTER | RIR | Mechanistic |
| Compulsive ORBIT | CED | Neutral, specific |
| Integrated ORBIT | AEE | Specifies adaptive quality |
| ORBIT vectors | Externalization vectors (EV) | Clearer |
| Authenticity stability | Subsumed under SF | Reduces proliferation |
| Self-continuity | Subsumed under IRC | Reduces proliferation |

# 2. Core Definitions

## 2.1 Internal Regulation Capacity (IRC)

Maximum capacity to maintain regulatory coherence internally under unresolved activation without requiring immediate external input.

Includes: tolerance of unresolved activation, identity coherence under stress, ability to delay externalization.

Does NOT include: emotional suppression (RIR), independence from others, absence of distress.

## 2.2 External Regulation Dependence (ERD)

Degree to which psychological stability requires external regulatory input. Dimensional, not categorical. Becomes structurally significant when external input is necessary for coherence rather than supportive of it.

## 2.3 Structural Flexibility (SF)

Capacity to hold tension, contradiction, and reality contact while shifting between strategies without fragmentation or rigidity.

Components:
- Tension processing: transform held activation over time
- Contradiction holding: sustain incompatible realities simultaneously
- Reality contact: perceive and remain with what is actually happening
- Regulatory flexibility: shift between internal and external regulation adaptively

## 2.4 Tension

Unresolved activation requiring regulatory processing.

Subclasses: attachment tension, identity tension, contradiction tension, shame tension, control tension.

## 2.5 Mirroring

External reflective input that alters perceived self-coherence, identity stability, or emotional legitimacy. Distinct from ordinary feedback: feedback informs behavior; mirroring modifies self-state stability.

## 2.6 REL

Time interval between unresolved activation and initiation of external regulatory behavior. Process metric at state/strategy level.

## 2.7 Externalization Vectors

Channels through which ERD operates. Three canonical vectors:
- **Validation**: stability through external confirmation
- **Dominance**: stability through environmental predictability
- **Shame-defense**: stability through projection/distortion of destabilizing affect

| Architecture | Habitual organization | Months–years | Chronic ERD |
| Capacity | Maximum competence | Developmental | IRC, SF |

# 4. The IRC × SF Space

The two capacity dimensions (IRC and SF) create a continuous two-dimensional space. For heuristic purposes, four regions of this space can be identified — these are not fixed categories but descriptive labels for regions of a continuous landscape:

| | Low SF | High SF |
|---|---|---|
| **High IRC** | RIR region: tends toward rigidity, suppression | IIR region: tends toward flexibility, integration |
| **Low IRC** | CED region: tends toward compulsive externalization | AEE region: tends toward adaptive engagement |

Individuals are not located permanently in one region. Movement across the space is expected depending on activation level, context, and developmental change. The labels describe tendencies within regions, not fixed personality positions.

# 5. Core Regulatory Model

**R = f(IRC, SF, EV, REL, S)**

Minimal conceptual architecture. Not exhaustive of all factors (temperament, physiology, etc.).

| Condition | Predicted pattern |
|---|---|
| Low IRC + low SF + short REL | CED |
| High IRC + high SF + short REL | May reflect AEE if non-compulsive |
| High IRC + low SF + long REL | RIR |
| High IRC + high SF + long REL | IIR |

Falsified if: IRC and SF prove indistinguishable, REL shows no independent value, vectors produce no differential predictions.

---

# 6. Testable Hypotheses

H1: Lower IRC → shorter REL, independent of attachment anxiety and distress tolerance.
H2: Higher SF correlates with flexibility, not suppression.
H3: Different vectors → different behavioral responses under different activation contexts.
H4: IRC and SF show partial but not complete correlation.
H5: RIR distinguishable from IIR through suppression measures.

---

# 7. Assessment: Gains and Losses

## Gains
- Clearer construct boundaries (IRC ≠ SF)
- No moral loading
- Orthogonal structure explicit
- Reduced construct proliferation

## Losses
- Loss of memorable labels (CENTER/ORBIT)
- Loss of poetic resonance for popular communication
- Increased jargon density
- Harder to explain to non-academic audiences

## Recommendation

Use IRC/ERD/SF for academic papers.
Retain CENTER/ORBIT for popular communication with explicit note that these are mnemonic labels.
