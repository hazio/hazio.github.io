# Core thesis

When psychologically relevant reality becomes too destabilizing to represent at sufficient resolution, the system may reconstruct accessible subjective reality in ways that preserve regulatory stability.

This is not merely deception.

It is defensive structural reconstruction.

Short form:

> Some minds do not merely defend against painful reality.
> They defend by restructuring reality itself.

---

# Distinction from ordinary lying

Critical distinction.

---

## Deliberate deception

Definition:

The person recognizes reality accurately but intentionally communicates false information.

Structure:

    reality intact
    truth known
    false communication chosen

Examples:

- strategic lying
- manipulation
- concealment

---

## Defensive reality reconstruction

Definition:

Accessible subjective reality itself becomes altered because accurate representation is too destabilizing.

Structure:

    reality threatening
    representation destabilizing
    reconstruction occurs
    altered narrative becomes accessible truth

Examples:

- confabulation
- narrative distortion
- contradiction-resistant reinterpretation
- emotionally driven false certainty

---

This distinction is essential.

Paper 7 primarily explains the second.
